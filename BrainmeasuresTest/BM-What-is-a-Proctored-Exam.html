What is a Proctored Exam?

The old assessment techniques have experienced considerable alterations in the constantly changing field of education. An example of this progression is the beginning of proctored exams. Proctored exams are becoming more popular as educational institutions adopt technology and realize the necessity for safe and equitable means of assessment. This article examines proctored tests, including their definition, use, types, benefits, drawbacks, and technological underpinnings.

Defining Proctored Exams

A proctored exam is a type of evaluation in which an authorized supervisor, also referred to as a proctor, supervises the examination procedure to guarantee its validity and integrity. The proctor's duties include monitoring test takers, guarding against cheating, and overseeing a regulated testing environment. Proctored tests can be administered digitally using online proctoring solutions or in person at places like testing centers

Why are Exams Proctored?

Proctored exams are primarily used to maintain the academic integrity of assessments. Educational institutions use proctored exams to ensure students display their knowledge and skills impartially and fairly. Institutions try to stop plagiarism, impersonation, and other academic dishonesty by having a proctor oversee the exam procedure.
